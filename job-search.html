Planning Your Job and Internship Search

What this page helps you do

  • Choose target roles and search channels.
  • Set a weekly routine you can actually sustain.
  • Track applications so you never lose deadlines or follow-ups.
  • Prepare materials and networking touchpoints that support each application.

Start here: a 60-minute setup

  1. Pick 1–2 target roles. Write them down (examples: UX Research Intern, Data Analyst Intern, Product Manager Intern).
  2. Collect 3 job/internship postings. Copy the “required skills” into a notes document and highlight repeated keywords.
  3. Update your resume for alignment. Use the same language you see in postings (skills, tools, responsibilities).
  4. Set up a tracking system. Use a simple table (template below) to track status and follow-ups.

A realistic weekly plan

Choose a routine you can repeat. Consistency matters more than intensity.

  • 2 applications per week that match your target roles.
  • 2 networking touches per week (message an alum, attend an event, or request an informational chat).
  • 1 skill-building block per week (course module, tutorial, small project, or portfolio update).
  • 15 minutes weekly review to update your tracker and plan next steps.

Common issues and quick fixes

  • Problem: Applying to too many unrelated roles.
    Fix: Narrow to 1–2 target roles for 2 weeks, then evaluate results.
  • Problem: Generic resume for every application.
    Fix: Customize the top third of your resume and 3 bullets for each role.
  • Problem: No follow-up plan.
    Fix: Add “next action + date” for every application in your tracker.
